About me

I got my first computer in 1983. It was a Dragon 32, and I remember playing chess on it, and a game where the object was to dive for treasures without being eaten by sharks. I soon upgraded to a Commodore 128, and spent my childhood playing computer games and sharing the casette tapes(!) with friends. I was definately mostly a consumer of tech, and didn't create much, neither code or woodworking. Although I remember trying to learn programming with a language called Turbo Pascal.

Ever since then, I have been very interested in technology and computers, but I never planned a carrer working in tech.

Instead I studied Psychology, Religion and Social Anthropology. I traveled to Asia and tried life as a Buddhist monk in Thailand and Bhutan

Eventually I managed to get a Masters degree in Social Anthropology, specializing in the topic of Buddhism in Bhutan.

I was fortunate enough to be able to stay in this wonderful monastery in Bhutan called Tango Gompa. I lived there for a year, and sometimes still regret ever leaving.

After a brief return to Norway, I again went out to teach Social Anthropology and Religious Studies in southern India for a year. This was in 2004, the year of the terrible tsunami that destroyed the coastlines all over south Asia.

After returning to Norway with my wife, I started to work with resettlement of refugees. After a while I got involved with the development of software for the Norwegian Directorate of Integration and Diversity (Imdi).

During my 11 years there I managed to get a Bachelors degree in Informatics from NTNU, and established the Test Manager position in Imdi. I started work as a Testing Consultant in Bouvet in 2019.

This Website

This website is made using only HTML and CSS. This was a real challenge. I had to go back to actually thinking about what I wanted the CSS to do, and why things were not looking the way I wanted them to.

I wanted to keep it simple, including the design. I chose a color palette with black, whitesmoke and grey, and a simple Arial font

It took some googleing, but I managed to make both the sticky menu, and these modals without resorting to javascript or css-libraries like Bootstrap
